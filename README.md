# Detecting-Emerging-Research-Trends-in-AI-Literature
Built an end-to-end pipeline to automatically analyze large-scale AI research trends using statistical methods and topic modeling.

The artificial intelligence (AI) research field is changing faster than ever before. New paradigms, like large language models (LLMs) and diffusion-based generative models, can go from being niche to being the most popular in just a few quarters. This makes it impossible to manually track trends on a large scale. This paper presents a system with multi-Keyword Temporal Analysis with Topic Drift Detection, an automated pipeline that (i) collects structured metadata for thousands of AI papers from the arXiv repository using a curated 150-term multi-keyword taxonomy, (ii) identifies latent research topics through Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) on abstract text, (iii) applies the non-parametric Mann-Kendall test to categorize each keyword as emerging, declining, or stable over quarterly time intervals, and (iv) detects major change in research focus by analyz- ing the Jensen-Shannon (JS) divergence between consecutive topic distributions. The system is checkpoint-safe, fully reproducible, and outputs structured data (CSV and JSON) that can be used in bibliometric or retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines. The experiments conducted on over 3,000 papers which were published from 2019 and 2024 identify 12 coherent research clusters, 23 statistically significant emerging keywords (e.g., instruction tuning, diffusion model, mixture of experts), and 8 topic drift events, notably a significant shift towards foundation models starting in the Q3 2022.
